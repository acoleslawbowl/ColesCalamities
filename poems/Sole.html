Sole

Cole Stanton

June 30th, 2026

Above, a cloudy starry sky
Around are countless
Who effortlessly shine
You wonder,
Why do I yearn so much
To shine just as they do?

So you sit up
And you walk
Amongst the stars
You think,
Maybe if I could
Get to know one
Maybe then
I could understand

Past lone stars
And constellations
Alike and yet not so
The clouds darken
And then part on your step
Yet you find
Something holds you back

You ponder why?
You say,
Those stars
Never parted the clouds
To form a constellation
Or maybe they have
Regardless
Who are you
To do such?
A comet
Who parts the clouds
Never finds it
Before it gives itself to stardust

So you retreat
Above, a starless sky
You squeeze your eyelids tight
This sky
Seems indistinguishable
From the one you put yourself under
And yet, this one is yours

You take your right hand
Your knuckles bleed
Glass panes shatter
You swear that
If enough shatters
Then maybe
You could be a star

Anger consumes you
Frustration seizes your heart
You pray
As a non-believer
Not to shine
But to fit somewhere
In that blind starry sky

But, most comets
Are missed
And under monochrome skies
The trees
Don’t care
You swear that yearning
Could have never burned
Not like this

Your knuckles turn numb
Your jabs slow
You realize
That you can never be a star
Even your past self wasn’t a star

And yet,
you continue to burn up
The clouds can’t hold
Your stardust
Much longer
