Do You Understand?

Cole Stanton

July 2nd, 2026

I don’t know
When I noticed,
What first
Pulled my gaze to you?
Was it when you were seated
Or when you stood
To catch the sea breeze?
Perhaps, neither
Probably, both

This strange thing
Which cuts through
Patted by gliding waves
It’s motions
Stretch and twist
My body churns
It finds it does not agree
With my frantic mind
Not too dissimilar
From the way you
Cause chaos
Cutting and patting
At my brain
Wanting to take in more
Only to throw it away

But you wouldn’t know
And won’t ever
No, I tell myself
Who is she to you?
She is but an object,
Perhaps a beautiful marble cliff
Which doesn’t weather
Against harsh waves
Or a delicate crocus
Surrounded by moss
And flower friends
Which drinks in sunlight
In a pot
Untarnished
By feet
And vitalized
By hands

Wouldn’t you like to know?
Some voice says,
Rudely, mockingly
A past self
Might have bit back
Out of spite
However experience tells
Me, you,
That I deserve that rudeness
If nothing else

But then the wind
Blows strands
Across your face
The waves seem to settle
And I forget
I ask
How it is fair
You look like this
While the rest of us
Something other?

It is rude, weird
Too look often
So I find a familiar face
It is not long
Before it slips past
And your deep brown eyes
Which twinkle in the sunshine
Are already on mine
And hold for
One, Two, Three
Mine flee first
They always did

Did you
Think I was pretty
The same way I thought you?
Now, I won’t ever know
Maybe you wonder the same
Maybe you already
Know your answer

Still, not so still
Are the waves
That lap at my chest
And threaten to drown
Like the voice,
That says,
You don’t understand
How beautiful
You are
Yet undoubtedly
One day
Someone will help you
Not me
No, never me
Not even fate
Can save me
