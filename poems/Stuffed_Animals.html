Stuffed Animals

Cole Stanton

July 9th, 2026

Your mother
Hands you
A feathery heap
With wide curious eyes
And small hands
You grab it,
Turn it around
And gasp
‘It’s an elephant!’
‘It’s an ostrich!’
‘It’s a dinosaur!’
Maybe, it’ll be more

Your mother
Pats your silken head
Tender, gently caring eyes
How could she forget
That look in your eyes
She won’t
‘Don’t hug it’
She’ll say,
‘Too much time,
And you’ll
Become it.’
Her words
Waft up and over
And past
Your innocent mind
Still, your grip loosens

The sky
Swathes into pinks, oranges
To darkness,
And back to blues
And over again
You wake,
Your feathery friend
Takes your hand
Leads you
Through empty lands
Your rapid feet
Slap against hardwood,
And then are caught by carpet
Your grip tightens

It isn’t long
Before you find
A stranger
With a friendly tone
Only, that’s not
Your mother’s voice
Still, they care, feed you
So maybe it isn’t so bad
Your grip tightens
It isn’t so bad
It isn’t so bad

The stranger
Never becomes
A familiar
Her voice changes
And each time,
‘That’s not
My mother’s voice’
It doesn’t grow,
It twists, turns
It deforms
It’s not gentle
It’s grotesque
Your grip tightens
You swear your grip tightens

A voice
You once thought
Was tender
Is no longer
Less strange
Than the strangers
‘My friend wouldn’t say that’
Your grip tightens
Like a reaffirmation
‘No he wouldn’t’

Your tears now
Aren’t shed
For the absence
Of a voice
Were they ever?
Uncertainty fills your chest
You swear not
The stranger speaks to you
Becomes frustrated at you
She scolds you
An older you
Jabs a finger at the glass pane
Your arms tighten
Close against your chest
Your vision blurs
This wasn’t your fault

Your hands move around
Grabbing, tossing clothes
Into a suitcase
In front of you
When your dull hands
Close around
A feathery heap
A hollow feeling
Washes over you
